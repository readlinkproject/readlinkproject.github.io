Our Story & Goals

Every child deserves the chance to discover the worlds hidden inside books. However, barriers to literacy still impact countless neighborhoods, leaving brilliant children behind before they even get a fair start.

A Note From Our Founder: "While volunteering at an elementary school robotics class, we handed out name tags to get to know the children. When I showed one student their tag to double-check their name, they looked up at me and softly said, 'I can't read.'

I was deeply shocked that a child thriving in a hands-on STEM setting couldn't read their own name. Literacy is a crucial foundation for all learning and life. That moment made it clear that I couldn't just stand by. I had to step up and build a solution."

That is how ReadLink Project was born. Our core goal is to close the resource gap, empowering young readers by fostering a supportive culture where basic education is accessible, interactive, and entirely free.

Our Core Mission

To construct accessible links between compassionate mentors and bright young minds, ensuring no child is left in the dark.

What We Aim To Do

Accessible Classrooms

Providing free reading workshops, structured lessons, and tailored academic support to children in underserved areas.

Literacy Resources

Distributing books and learning materials straight into communities, ensuring no child goes without a story to read.

Empowering Opportunities

Creating bright pipelines for mentorship, continuous learning, and community engagement to help kids thrive inside and outside school.
